Strategic Proposal for BNJC Leadership

Language. Community. Culture. A New Model for Connection.

A year-long program offering broad educational and community infrastructure for BNJC: from spaces for dialogue, leisure, and enrichment for adults, to pedagogical training of the young staff.

Vision & Need Staff Training Curriculum
Program Philosophy

Not Just a Language — a Way of Life

Hebrew in a community center cannot be taught in a vacuum. The program is built on a synergistic connection between three nodes that feed one another:

A Living Language

Moving from passive memorization to active use. Focus on spoken, practical Hebrew that gives participants confidence in natural conversation.

Cohesive Community

Building an intergenerational bridge among learners of all ages, turning the learning experience into a social space that actively brings participants closer to BNJC.

Cultural Anchor

Intellectual and emotional connection through cuisine, cinema, music, and current events. Using the language as an engaging window into Israeli life.

Systemic Implementation: To ensure all three nodes are expressed across all activities, the program is built in a two-phase model — first establishing the professional foundation (young staff training), then building adult content programs integrated into the center's spaces.

Phase A

Staff Training & Long-Term Infrastructure

Elevating the professional level of young staff and creating educational assets that will remain at BNJC for years to come.

Creative Pedagogy Workshops

Professional guidance for young staff in experiential teaching methods: gamification, learning through songs, public speaking, and effective facilitation of children's groups.

Building an "Activity Bank"

Creating an organized digital repository of modular lesson plans. Significantly eases staff workload and ensures the center maintains high quality even during staff transitions.

Phase B

"Hebrew as a Lifestyle" — The Adult Program

A three-tier model enabling the center to offer different solutions for different target audiences, making optimal use of the building's facilities.

1

The Classic Ulpan

For Beginners & Intermediate

Structured, consistent learning of language foundations, grammar, and vocabulary in a weekly session with a supportive atmosphere.

2

Hebrew Hub

For the General Public — in the Café

An open monthly community event. An informal "Hour of Hebrew" that increases traffic to the BNJC café.

3

In-Depth Lectures

For Advanced & Native Speakers

Focused lectures in rich Hebrew on topics such as literature, current events, and history, positioning the center as an intellectual hub.
